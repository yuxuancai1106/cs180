Project 0

Part 1: Selfie — The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

How camera distance and zoom affect portrait distortion. A close-up selfie produces a distorted look, while stepping back and zooming in produces a more natural result. Both portraits are roughly the same size but look very different.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Demonstrating perspective compression in an urban setting. A zoomed-in shot flattens the scene, while walking closer with no zoom restores depth and makes the geometry feel more natural.

Part 3: The Dolly Zoom

Reproducing the famous dolly zoom effect. Moving the camera backward while zooming in makes the background warp while the subject stays the same size.
